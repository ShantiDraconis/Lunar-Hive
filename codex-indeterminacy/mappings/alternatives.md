# Alternative Interpretations

## Purpose

Documents alternative interpretations for symbols, structures, and patterns to maintain multiple competing hypotheses.

## Principle

For every proposed interpretation, maintain at least 2-3 plausible alternatives until evidence excludes them.

## Symbol Function Alternatives

### High-Frequency Symbols

**Interpretation A:** Phonetic elements (common syllables/phonemes)
**Interpretation B:** Grammatical markers (particles, affixes)
**Interpretation C:** Common words (articles, prepositions)

**Evidence needed:** Distributional analysis, comparative patterns

### Low-Frequency Symbols

**Interpretation A:** Rare words, specialized vocabulary
**Interpretation B:** Personal names, place names
**Interpretation C:** Scribal errors or variants

**Evidence needed:** Context analysis, error patterns

## Text Structure Alternatives

### Seal Texts

**Model A:** Personal identification (Name + Title)
**Model B:** Trade label (Commodity + Quantity + Source)
**Model C:** Administrative record (Subject + Verb + Object)
**Model D:** Single compound word or phrase

**Current status:** All remain possible, insufficient evidence to exclude any.

## Writing System Alternatives

**System A:** Logo-syllabic mix (like Sumerian)
**System B:** Pure syllabary (like Linear B)
**System C:** Abjad/Abugida (consonants primary)
**System D:** Pure logographic (like early Chinese)

**Evidence:** Symbol count suggests A or B most likely, but others not excluded.

---

*Maintain alternatives until falsified.*
