# Unresolved Symbols

## Purpose

Documents symbols and symbol patterns that resist interpretation or classification.

## Principle

**Open questions are valuable.** They mark boundaries of current understanding and guide future research.

## Categories of Unresolved Symbols

### Hapax Legomena
Symbols appearing only once in corpus.

**Challenge:** Single occurrence provides minimal context.

**Questions:**
- Are these rare words, names, or scribal errors?
- Do they represent regional variants?
- Are they later additions to symbol inventory?

**Status:** Cannot determine function from single occurrence.

### Ambiguous Forms
Symbols that may be variants of others or distinct signs.

**Challenge:** Scribal variation vs. distinct symbols.

**Questions:**
- Where to draw boundaries between variants?
- Are distinctions meaningful (phonetic/semantic) or aesthetic?
- How much variation is acceptable?

**Status:** Classification uncertain, requires judgment calls.

### Position-Ambiguous Symbols
Symbols appearing in multiple positions with no clear pattern.

**Challenge:** No distributional clue to function.

**Questions:**
- Are these highly versatile functional elements?
- Or do they represent multiple homophones?
- Or is apparent randomness artifact of small sample?

**Status:** Function unclear.

### Complex Compounds
Multi-element symbols that may be single signs or combinations.

**Challenge:** Segmentation uncertainty.

**Questions:**
- Should complex symbol be parsed as X+Y or as single Z?
- Do scribes treat them as units or combinations?
- Do they have compositional meaning or fixed meaning?

**Status:** Segmentation disputed.

## Why Some Symbols Resist Resolution

### Insufficient Context
- Corpus too small
- Texts too short
- Contexts too similar (seal texts only)

### Preservation Issues
- Degraded forms
- Ambiguous impressions
- Damaged inscriptions

### Genuine Ambiguity
- Scribal freedom in symbol formation
- Regional/temporal variation
- Multiple correct forms

### Methodological Limits
- Current tools insufficient
- Pattern recognition at limits
- Human cognitive biases

## Open Research Questions

1. How to distinguish variants from distinct symbols?
2. What confidence level for symbol identification?
3. Are hapax legomena errors, names, or rare terms?
4. Can statistical methods help resolve ambiguous cases?
5. Will larger corpus resolve current ambiguities or create new ones?

## Strategy for Unresolved Cases

### Document, Don't Decide
- Note ambiguity explicitly
- Provide multiple interpretations
- Assign low confidence
- Flag for future review

### Track Over Time
- Do more examples emerge?
- Do patterns become clearer?
- Can new methods help?

### Accept Irreducibility
- Some cases may never resolve
- Indeterminacy may be permanent
- This is intellectually honest position
